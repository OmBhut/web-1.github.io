*Sardar Vallabh Bhai Patel*

Coordinates 21.8380°N 73.7191°E
Location Narmada Valley Kevadiya, Narmada, Gujarat, India
Designer Ram V. Sutar
Type Statue
Material Steel framing, reinforced by concrete and brass coating, bronze cladding[1]
Height 182 meters (597 ft)
Visitors 2.8 million[2] (in 2018-19)
Beginning date 31 October 2013
Completion date 30 October 2018
Opening date 31 October 2018
Dedicated to Vallabhbhai Patel

History

  • Narendra Modi announced the project on 7 October 2013 to mark the beginning of his tenth year as the chief minister of Gujarat[7].
  • A society named Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Rashtriya Ekta Trust (SVPRET) was formed under the chairmanship of the Chief Minister of Gujarat, to execute the project.
  • Statue of Unity Movement was started in 2013 to collect the iron needed for the statue by asking farmers to donate their used farming instruments. By 2016, a total of 135 metric tonnes of scrap iron had been collected and about 109 tonnes of it was used to make the foundation of the statue after processing. A marathon titled Run For Unity was held on 15 December 2013 in Surat and Vadodara in support of the project.

Features

    A Selfie Point:

  • One can enjoy a good view of the surrounding from the selfie point at the statue. A museum and audio-video gallery are also a major attraction. One can get the information of the unification of India after independence from a laser light show.
    •

    Statue of Unity Ticket Price And Booking:

  • There are two categories for ticket prices. For the children between 3 to 15 years, the cost of the ticket is half, and for the adult, the cost of a ticket is full. With this ticket, you can see the Observation Deck view, Memorial, Valley of Flower, SOU site, Museum & Audio Visual Gallery, and Sardar Sarovar Dam.
    •

    Ticket Booking and Timing:

  • It is open all weekdays from 9 am to 6 pm. As I already said the prices of tickets are Rs 350 and RS 200 for Adult and kids between 3 to 15 respectively.
    •

    Statue of Unity Entry Ticket:

  • It is another ticket option that you can choose if you want. It is an entry ticket, and it charges 120 RS and 60 RS for adult and children aged between 3 to 15 years respectively.
    •

    Statue of Unity Bus Ticket:

  • The bus ticket costs RS 30, but you do not have to pay for it if you have already booked tickets of Entry ticket or Observation Deck Ticket as both include the bus tickets in it.
  • You can take a ticket form the place or book it using a debit card or net banking or debit card, and for that, you don’t have to pay extra charges. But if you pick the credit card option to pay, then you have to pay 1% + GST on the total amount.
    •

Tourism

  • Over 128,000 tourists visited the statue in the 11 days after its opening to the public on 1 November 2018.
  • The daily average tourist footfall at Statue of Unity during November 2019 reached 15,036, outpacing the Statue of Liberty (which attracts around 10,000 daily visitors on average).
  • It has been included in the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation’s ‘8 Wonders of SCO’ list.
  • In its first year of operation, the Statue of Unity attracted 2,900,000 visitors and collected ₹82 crore (US$11.64 million) in ticket revenue.
  • By 15 March 2021, 5,000,000 tourists visited the venue.
